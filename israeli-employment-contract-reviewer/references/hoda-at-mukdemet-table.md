# Hoda'at Mukdemet (Advance Notice) Statutory Table

Source: Prior Notice of Dismissal and Resignation Law, 5761-2001 (`חוק הודעה מוקדמת לפיטורים ולהתפטרות, התשס"א-2001`).

Days are calendar days, not business days. Weekends and holidays count.

## Monthly Employees (oved chodshi - paid a fixed monthly salary)

| Tenure | Advance notice days | Notes |
|--------|---------------------|-------|
| 1 month | 1 day | 1 day per month of work |
| 2 months | 2 days | |
| 3 months | 3 days | |
| 4 months | 4 days | |
| 5 months | 5 days | |
| 6 months | 6 days | End of initial phase |
| 7 months | 8.5 days | 6 + 2.5 per additional month |
| 8 months | 11 days | |
| 9 months | 13.5 days | |
| 10 months | 16 days | |
| 11 months | 18.5 days | |
| 12 months | 21 days | Last month of year 1 |
| 12+ months (1+ year) | 30 days (1 month) | Fixed after year 1 |

Phase 1 (months 1-6): 1 day per month of work
Phase 2 (months 7-12): 6 days + 2.5 days for each month beyond the 6th
Phase 3 (after year 1): 30 calendar days, flat

## Hourly / Daily Employees (oved sha'ati / yomi)

| Tenure | Advance notice days |
|--------|---------------------|
| Month 1-6 of year 1 | 1 day per month |
| Year 1 full | 6 days |
| Year 2 | 12 days |
| Year 2 + 2.5 days per month after | up to 30 |
| Year 3+ | 30 days |

Phase 1: 1 day per month in months 1-6
Phase 2 (from month 7 onward): 2.5 days per month
Phase 3 (after 3 years): 30 calendar days, flat

## Key Rules

- **Calendar days, not business days**. A 30-day notice means 30 actual days.
- **Applies to both sides**. Employee resigning and employer firing are both bound by the same table.
- **Written notice required**. Verbal notice is legally questionable and hard to prove.
- **Payment in lieu**. Employer can pay the equivalent of the notice period instead of requiring the employee to work through it (hoda'at mukdemet tashlum bedineha).
- **No notice during first month of probation**. Some exceptions exist for very early termination.

## Examples

| Scenario | Required notice |
|----------|----------------|
| Monthly employee, 2 years tenure, fired | 30 days |
| Monthly employee, 3 months tenure, resigning | 3 days |
| Monthly employee, 9 months tenure, fired | 13.5 days (round up) |
| Hourly employee, 1.5 years, fired | approx 21 days |
| Monthly employee, 11 years, fired | 30 days (flat after year 1) |

## How to Use This in Contract Review

When reviewing a contract, find the advance notice clause. Check:

1. Does the contract's notice match or exceed the statutory minimum for the expected tenure?
2. Does the contract distinguish employer notice and employee notice? (They should be the same.)
3. Is the notice in "days" without specifying calendar vs business? (Prefer explicit "calendar days.")
4. Is there a cap on employer-paid notice ("payment in lieu")? (Standard: yes.)

Any notice period lower than the statutory minimum is unenforceable against the employee (because cogent labor law cannot be waived), but it signals that the employer is not aware of Israeli law, which is a broader warning sign.
